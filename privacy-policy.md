# Privacy Policy for Compose Assistant

*Last updated: February 14, 2026*

## What We Collect

Compose Assistant collects the following data to provide AI-powered writing assistance:

- **Account information**: Name and email address via Google Sign-In, used for authentication and personalizing drafts.
- **Text field content**: The content of the text field you are actively composing in is sent to our server to generate AI-assisted drafts. This includes the surrounding page context (e.g., conversation thread) needed to produce contextually relevant responses.
- **Page metadata**: The URL and page title of the site you are composing on, used to determine platform-specific formatting (e.g., LinkedIn vs Gmail).
- **User preferences**: Your selected LLM provider, model, and API keys (if using Bring Your Own Key), stored locally in your browser.

## How We Use Your Data

- Text content is sent to our server and processed by AI language models (Google Gemini) solely to generate writing suggestions.
- Account information is used to maintain your personal memory context so the assistant can provide personalized drafts.
- We do not sell, share, or transfer your data to third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

## Data Storage

- User preferences and authentication state are stored locally in your browser via Chrome Storage.
- Writing context (memories) is stored in Supermemory, isolated per user account. Each user can only access their own data.
- Text content sent for draft generation is not permanently stored on our servers.

## Data Security

- All data is transmitted over HTTPS (encrypted in transit).
- Each user's data is isolated by unique user ID.
- Google OAuth tokens are used transiently and not persisted on our servers.

## Your Rights

- You can delete your stored memories at any time through the extension's Memories viewer.
- You can sign out at any time, which stops all data collection.
- Uninstalling the extension removes all locally stored data.
